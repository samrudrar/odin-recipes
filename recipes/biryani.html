Mutton Biryani

(Traditional Dum-Style)



Home

Ingredients

For Marinating Mutton:

  • 1 kg mutton (bone-in, cleaned)
  • 1 cup thick yogurt (curd)
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons biryani masala
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • ½ cup fried onions (birista)
  • Handful of chopped mint leaves
  • Handful of chopped coriander leaves

For Rice:

  • 4 cups basmati rice
  • Water for boiling
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4 green cardamoms
  • 4 cloves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 tablespoon salt

For Biryani Layering:

  • ¼ cup ghee
  • ¼ cup milk
  • A pinch of saffron strands
  • Extra fried onions
  • Extra mint and coriander leaves

Directions:

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.

  2. In a large bowl, combine mutton, yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, biryani masala, lemon juice, salt, fried onions, mint, and coriander. Mix well, cover, and marinate for at least 2 hours (overnight preferred).

  3. Heat a heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat and add ghee. Add the marinated mutton and cook for 10 to 15 minutes until oil separates and the mutton is partially cooked.

  4. In a separate large pot, bring water to a rolling boil. Add bay leaves, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and salt. Add soaked rice and cook until 70% done. Drain immediately.

  5. Lower the heat on the mutton pot. Spread half of the rice evenly over the mutton.

  6. Sprinkle fried onions, mint, coriander, and saffron milk on top. Add the remaining rice and repeat the garnishing.

  7. Cover the pot tightly with a lid (seal with dough if possible). Cook on very low heat (dum) for 30 to 40 minutes until the mutton is tender and the rice is fully cooked.

  8. Turn off the heat and let the biryani rest for 10 minutes.

  9. Gently fluff the rice from the sides and serve hot with raita or salan.

  10. Enjoy!
